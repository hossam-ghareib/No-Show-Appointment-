Introduction¶

This Dataset is collected from a medical appointment in Brazil and is focused on the Show or no-show appointment of the patient and they are 100k medical appointment from different hospitals in Brazil and consistes of 14 columns each column show a part of the patient history, age,and chronic diseases.

1. Dataset Discription:¶

  1. Patient ID: The identification for each Patient.
  2. Appointment ID: The identification for each appointment.
  3. Gender: The coloumns haver a Male (M) or a Female (F) dataset.
  4. Scheduled Day: The date of the patient setup their appointment.
  5. Appointment Day: The date that the patient will show to the doctor.
  6. Age: Shows The age of the patient.
  7. Neighbourhood: Indecates the location of the hosptial.
  8. Scholarship: Indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brazil welfare program [Bolsa Familia].
  9. Hipertension: Ture Or False.
  10. Diabetes: True Or False
  11. Alcoholism: True Or False.
  12. Handicap: if the patient had a hadicap (True),if not (False).
  13. SMS Received: Is the patient receive an sms the columns have zeros and ones (zero = False, One = True).
  14. NoShow: Represents the status of the patient (If the patient didn't come the column has "Yes",or If the patient came "No").

def health_condition_plot(df):

    # Groupine the data by attendance and sum the health condition columns

    health_conditions.plot(kind='bar')

Conclusions¶

Answer the research question¶

Based on the visualizations, some of the important factors to consider in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment are age, SMS reminders, health conditions, and time of appointment. Specifically, the data shows that younger patients tend to have a higher attendance rate, patients who receive SMS reminders tend to have a higher attendance rate, patients with certain health conditions (such as diabetes and hypertension) tend to have a lower attendance rate, and appointments scheduled in the morning tend to have a higher attendance rate compared to appointments scheduled in the afternoon or evening. Therefore, these factors could be considered when developing a prediction model to determine the likelihood of a patient showing up for their scheduled appointment.

Limitations of the Dataset:¶

The limitations of the dataset include the fact that it only includes information about appointments in a specific region of Brazil, only includes appointments from a certain time period, does not include information about certain variables that may be important for predicting patient attendance, and may be subject to selection bias.
